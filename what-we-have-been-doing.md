# What Bitcoin Cash Argentina Has Done

For more than five years, Bitcoin Cash Argentina has worked continuously to expand the use, understanding, visibility, and practical utility of Bitcoin Cash throughout Argentina and the Spanish-speaking world. Its public activity dates back to [November 24, 2020](https://x.com/BCHArgentina/status/1331360570109538308).

Updated through July 21, 2026, this document consolidates the work recorded in BCH Argentina's public X history beginning in November 2020, its monthly reports from November 2021 through October 2025, and later public completion reports and evidence collected by the team. It groups recurring work and individual milestones by area, without separating them by funding campaign or repeating the same initiative every month.

## Adoption and merchant network

- We helped grow the Argentine BCH merchant network from only two verified businesses when the adoption effort began in March 2021 to [75 by July 2021](https://x.com/BCHArgentina/status/1410990589450100738), [95 later that month](https://x.com/BCHArgentina/status/1415444657593589768), roughly 200 in February 2022, 250 in May 2022, 275 in October 2022, [more than 300 in March 2023](https://read.cash/@BitcoinCashArgentina/300-merchants-accept-bch-in-argentina-fa3b117b), [350 in the following expansion](https://read.cash/@BitcoinCashArgentina/flipstarter-4-completed-9003a2d0), and [more than 400 shops and businesses](https://x.com/BCHArgentina/status/2016988208966320338).
- The first merchant publicly documented by the project was [Hotel Atlántida in Río Grande, Tierra del Fuego](https://x.com/BCHArgentina/status/1370757562426130434), announced on March 13, 2021. The early network quickly expanded across hospitality, food, retail, technology, professional services, sports, logistics, and online businesses.
- We developed a repeatable merchant-onboarding process covering commercial outreach, registration in the merchant database, welcome and training materials, promotion, follow-up, and recurring audits.
- We personally visit and verify important physical merchants, test transactions, train staff, correct payment procedures, and provide ongoing support so that listed businesses can reliably receive BCH.
- We perform recurring on-site and remote audits and maintain a visual last-activity system so users can distinguish recently verified businesses from listings that need review.
- We provide merchants and users with direct human support 24 hours a day, seven days a week.
- We distribute BCH acceptance stickers, QR materials, signs, illuminated signs, coasters, napkin rings, aprons, caps, T-shirts, guides, and other branded materials.
- We designed a new “BCH Accepted Here” sticker with a QR code leading directly to a wallet download.
- We produced custom payment aprons and caps for K24 employees, including merchant payment QRs and joint K24/BCH Argentina branding.
- We onboarded and supported 30 locations of the K24 convenience-store chain, trained employees, audited payment procedures, and explored BCH use cases involving suppliers, salaries, and remittances. [Read more](https://medium.com/@BCHArgentina/k24-convenience-store-chain-bch-argentina-c83c7aeddf86).
- The original K24 case study documented 30 active BCH locations within an 85-store network. Each location used its own BCH address, printed QR, and block explorer on the store's existing desktop computer, allowing adoption without requiring dedicated phones or tablets. [K24 case study](https://read.cash/@BitcoinCashArgentina/k24-convenience-store-chain-bch-argentina-1f3bb55d).
- We conducted a major K24/M24 audit after substantial employee turnover, retraining staff and restoring their ability to receive BCH correctly.
- We worked with online merchants to integrate direct BCH checkout through Prompt.Cash; [Akataka became the first participating store](https://x.com/BCHArgentina/status/1517629317047627778) and enabled customers anywhere to buy role-playing games with BCH.
- We helped Bitcoin.com and Prompt.Cash use a realistic market-based BCH/ARS rate instead of Argentina's restricted official exchange rate, removing a major barrier to local BCH payments. [Read more](https://read.cash/@Pantera/bitcoin-cash-argentina-a80dbfc8).
- We created the [BCH Corner](https://read.cash/@BitcoinCashArgentina/2-new-flipstarter-4-goals-completed-adoption-and-branding-merchandising-44a3e4f7), a prominent Buenos Aires corner where three bars and restaurants accept BCH.
- We equipped the BCH Corner with BCH branding, illuminated signs, screens, coasters, and other materials, and organized BCH Experience events there.
- We launched a merchant discount program to give participating businesses greater visibility and attract customers paying with BCH.
- Examples have included discounts at CheBonche, Sandoval, Akataka, and other participating businesses, with the offers later made visible directly in MAPA.
- We created a merchant network/community group focused on cooperation among businesses that accept BCH.
- In 2026, the merchant group evolved into an active community [Marketplace](https://x.com/BCHArgentina/status/2046242477686882492) where members can buy, sell, and exchange with one another using BCH.
- We publicized Lagunas Tolhuin, a sustainable real-estate development in Tierra del Fuego with 152 lots that accepted BCH, demonstrating that local BCH commerce extended from everyday purchases to property. [Read more](https://read.cash/@BitcoinCashArgentina/can-i-buy-a-property-with-bitcoin-cash-in-argentina-27f52d80).
- We offered BCH rewards for merchant audits and other forms of community participation.
- We organized recurring merchant-supported raffles in which prizes came from businesses accepting BCH. Examples: [1](https://www.instagram.com/p/DH_RU2_RMCO/), [2](https://www.instagram.com/p/DJ9-_yDxBb2/), [3](https://www.instagram.com/reel/DN6QSP-ERKn/), [4](https://www.instagram.com/p/DR5ViiUEWJa/), [5](https://www.instagram.com/p/DSnqGy7EVOZ/), [6](https://www.instagram.com/p/DUjXiyykRO0/), and [7](https://www.instagram.com/p/DVSCcjcDVWg/).

## Real economic use and circular economy

- We promoted real BCH commerce beyond isolated consumer purchases, including business-to-business transactions between merchants. [Public example](https://x.com/BCHArgentina/status/1697653030198419684).
- Libros Libertarios used BCH to pay a book supplier and then sold the books for BCH. [Public evidence](https://x.com/LibertariosBook/status/1544691528110673920).
- A BCH-accepting artist used a BCH-accepting delivery service to complete and ship a sale, creating a practical multi-business BCH transaction.
- A BCH-accepting cleaning and bazaar business hired a BCH-accepting graphic-design business and paid in BCH for its signage.
- A candy-store chain used a BCH-accepting graphic-design supplier and installed BCH materials across nine branches. [Public evidence](https://x.com/BCHArgentina/status/1701653710944846060).
- We documented BCH use at popular local fairs, showing adoption across informal and lower-cost segments of the economy as well as established retailers. [BCH Argentina publication](https://x.com/BCHArgentina/status/1670108230486294528) and [merchant publication](https://x.com/alan89dtom/status/1670161838510686209).
- We promoted first transactions and BCH-only discounts at new merchants so adoption translated into actual payment activity.
- We moderate an invitation-based BCH/fiat peer-to-peer exchange group with more than 100 members and, according to the reports, more than six exchanges per day.
- We worked with [PagoConBit](https://pagoconbit.com/), a project created by members of the local BCH community that lets users buy Argentine gift cards and pay services, taxes, and transportation using BCH.
- We held meetings with Kripton Market to discuss adding BCH to its marketplace for products and services.
- We exchanged merchant-onboarding knowledge and strategies with Bitcoin Cash City in Australia, connecting two major regional adoption initiatives. [Read more](https://read.cash/@alberdioni8406/bch-argentina-hits-300-businesses-taiwan-adds-another-accepting-bch-last-weeks-bch-activity-663c7180).

## Applications and digital infrastructure

- We launched [USAR](https://usar.bcharg.com/) as a BCH merchant marketplace, with merchant products, delivery or pickup options, and a connection to the CAJA platform. The original app was later paused while the team worked on a replacement.
- USAR allowed participating merchants to manage their own product listings, descriptions, prices, stock, delivery and pickup options, while receiving BCH payments directly. [USAR marketplace overview](https://read.cash/@BitcoinCashArgentina/usar-a-marketplace-for-bch-merchants-in-argentina-692ab9c5).
- We developed [caja.bcharg.com](https://caja.bcharg.com/), a non-custodial point-of-sale and reporting platform designed for businesses with multiple branches, managers, and cashiers. [Launch announcement](https://medium.com/@BCHArgentina/launch-of-the-beta-phase-of-caja-bcharg-com-6ca0ec878bcf).
- CAJA lets each branch use its own BCH address, receive payments to a printed QR, view incoming payments quickly, assign multiple cashiers, filter sales, and see branch-level or consolidated statistics.
- CAJA preserves the ARS value recorded at the moment of payment, helping businesses reconcile their cash registers without later exchange-rate movements changing their records.
- CAJA does not custody funds or charge a transaction commission: payments go directly to the business owner's wallet. Cashier accounts have read-only access to payments and metrics for their assigned branches, while managers receive branch-level and consolidated views. Its familiar interface was inspired by Mercado Pago and developed with direct feedback from the local business community. [CAJA beta launch](https://read.cash/@BitcoinCashArgentina/launch-of-the-beta-phase-of-cajabchargcom-481b9a8e).
- We tested CAJA with real merchants and recorded dozens of transactions during its private beta before opening it more broadly.
- In late 2025, we migrated CAJA's infrastructure to improve reliability, eliminated dashboard loading and server errors, strengthened blockchain payment synchronization, and implemented historical BCH/ARS and BCH/USD pricing so every recorded sale preserves the exchange rate at the exact moment of payment. [Infrastructure](https://x.com/BCHArgentina/status/2005018244336259197), [payment synchronization](https://x.com/BCHArgentina/status/2005018317158043942), and [historical pricing](https://x.com/BCHArgentina/status/2005018384174645272).
- We launched [MAPA 2.0](https://mapa.bcharg.com/), an updated platform for finding BCH merchants. [Launch announcement](https://read.cash/@BitcoinCashArgentina/launch-of-the-new-mapa-20-6cc6e0f1).
- MAPA includes map and list views, proximity search, merchant categories, business types, contact and directions actions, discounts, Spanish and English interfaces, and support for both physical and online stores.
- We added community-driven “last purchase” updates and a traffic-light status system: green for activity within three months, yellow for three to six months, and red for more than six months.
- We added filtering by recent activity, a “Report Store” function for outdated listings, discount badges, and embeddable map support for external websites and apps.
- MAPA distinguishes physical stores from online businesses, sorts merchants by proximity when location is enabled, supports keyword search, and lets community members update recent activity or report a listing without creating an account. [MAPA 2.0 details](https://read.cash/@BitcoinCashArgentina/launch-of-the-new-mapa-20-6cc6e0f1).
- After launching MAPA in Spanish and English, we expanded its language support to German, Filipino, Portuguese, French, Mandarin, Japanese, and additional languages. [Public evidence](https://x.com/BCHArgentina/status/2007596043937165560).
- In 2026, we acquired [BCHMap.org](https://bchmap.org/) and transformed the Argentine map into an [international Bitcoin Cash merchant map](https://x.com/BCHArgentina/status/2022088799128351145), with a simplified multilingual registration form and support for physical stores, in-person services, digital services, and hybrid businesses. [Registration and business-type update](https://x.com/BCHArgentina/status/2016268922824360335).
- We integrated [Paytaca's merchant listings into BCH Map](https://x.com/BCHArgentina/status/2035197124980220180), taking a concrete step toward coordinating merchant data with BCH communities in other countries and allowing local community leaders to manage their own listings.
- Community feedback collected during the BCH Argentina Club directly influenced MAPA: after a merchant proposed visible discounts, the feature was added.
- We launched a new [Bitcoin Cash Argentina website](https://bitcoincashargentina.com/) with educational resources, local information, merchant tools, and links for new users.
- The project's first website was [launched in March 2021](https://x.com/BCHArgentina/status/1366918066010005511) with the participation and support of Roger Ver. [Launch video](https://youtu.be/J5zP-ubUpXU).
- We updated the website with the conference aftermovie, BCH Argentina Club information, the local merchant map, wallet resources, and plans for an ARG CashToken section and easier BCH purchases.
- We created and maintain a merchant database, merchant-intake chatbot, and internal management dashboard for assigning and tracking team tasks.
- We first launched ARG as the official BCH Argentina SEP20 token on SmartBCH, using it to introduce local users to the BCH DeFi ecosystem. Two of the first community airdrops distributed ARG to [more than 4,100 people](https://x.com/BCHArgentina/status/1436374020660908051), alongside educational challenges, videos, and community activities. [Original ARG announcement and specifications](https://read.cash/@BitcoinCashArgentina/arg-the-official-token-of-the-bitcoin-cash-argentina-team-4f54b0e0).
- We later launched ARG as the official [Bitcoin Cash Argentina CashToken](https://x.com/BCHArgentina/status/1770075530265563392), extending the community token to the Bitcoin Cash main-chain CashTokens ecosystem and giving local users a first experience with CashTokens and BCH DeFi. Its CashToken TokenID is `ee9b7f700d17b31aa1ff8e9b58bed110ed9108cd2f548009dbd9c5702d80e1cc`. The token was also distributed through community airdrops and made available on BCH decentralized exchanges.
- We developed a community bot to support participation, reputation, and engagement in BCH groups. [Update 1](https://x.com/BCHArgentina/status/2054390576015917486) and [update 2](https://x.com/BCHArgentina/status/2053912870996377765).
